The Evolution of the Internet

An interactive timeline exploring key milestones in the development of the internet.

1969s 1983s 1989 1991s 1993 2004s 2007 2011 2014 2023

1969

ARPANET Created

The Advanced Research Projects Agency Network (ARPANET) was established by the U.S. Department of Defense. It was the first network to implement the protocol suite TCP/IP, which would later become the foundation of the modern internet.

1983

TCP/IP Protocol Suite Standardized

ARPANET adopted the TCP/IP protocol suite as the standard for data transmission. This crucial development allowed different kinds of computers on different networks to communicate with each other.

1989

Proposal for the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee, a British scientist at CERN, proposed a new project to create a distributed information system. This proposal would eventually lead to the creation of the World Wide Web.

1991

Launch of the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee launched the first website at CERN, marking the official birth of the World Wide Web. This site explained the basics of the web and how to use a browser.

1993

Release of Mosaic Browser

The Mosaic web browser, developed at the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA), was released. It was the first widely used web browser and significantly contributed to the internet's popularity.

2004

Facebook Launched

Mark Zuckerberg and his college roommates launched Facebook, initially limited to Harvard students. It quickly expanded to other universities and eventually became a global social media platform.

2007

Introduction of the iPhone

Apple released the first iPhone, revolutionizing mobile computing and internet access. The iPhone's success led to the widespread adoption of smartphones, making the internet more accessible than ever before.

2011

Launch of Google Cloud Platform

Google launched its cloud computing services, enabling businesses to build, test, and deploy applications on Google’s infrastructure. This marked a significant step in the growth of cloud computing.

2014

Growth of the Internet of Things

The concept of the Internet of Things (IoT) gained significant traction, with more devices than ever before being connected to the internet. This included everything from household appliances to cars, all capable of sending and receiving data.

2023

Expansion of 5G Networks

5G networks became widely available, offering significantly faster internet speeds and lower latency. This advancement paved the way for new technologies and services, including enhanced IoT capabilities, autonomous vehicles, and more immersive virtual reality experiences.
